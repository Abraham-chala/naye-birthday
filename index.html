Happy Birthday, Naye!

It all started with a few words someone said — words about a girl in Addis Ababa. Just a description. Just a name. But somehow, those words were enough to make a boy in Ginchi stop everything he was doing and go searching for you on Facebook.

Think about that for a moment. He had never seen you. Never spoken to you. But something about the way your name was mentioned made him feel like he had to find you. And he did.

That is not a coincidence. That is the kind of thing that only happens when two people are meant to cross paths — no matter how far apart they are.

Then a whole year passed. He had your number. He never called. Not because he forgot — but because some people mean so much to you that you are afraid to say the wrong thing. So he waited. And life, doing what life does, brought you to Ginchi anyway.

Today is your birthday, Naye. And on this day, all of those moments — the searching, the waiting, the introduction, every single conversation since — they all come together to say one thing:

You are someone worth finding. Worth waiting for. Worth celebrating. Happy Birthday — may this year bring you everything you deserve. 🎂

Grade 11 · Two Different Cities

He heard about her before he ever saw her

She was in Addis Ababa. He was in Ginchi. Someone mentioned her — just casually, just a few words about her beauty. But those words landed differently on him. He opened Facebook, typed her name, and searched until he found her. A boy looking for a girl he had never met — because something told him she was worth finding.

Grade 11 · The First Message

A bold "Hey" — and a promise she didn't believe yet

He sent the message. She replied with a simple "hey" back. He introduced himself and said something she probably didn't expect: "Give me your number — you'll fall in love within a month." She smiled, said "we'll see," and sent her number anyway. He saved it. And then — he never called. Not for an entire year. Because some people feel too important to rush.

Grade 12 · Fate Moves First

She came to Ginchi — and didn't even know it was him

A year later, she arrived in Ginchi. A mutual friend introduced them — as if they had never spoken before. She had no idea this was the same boy from Facebook. He knew exactly who she was. He had always known. They started talking again, this time face to face, and every conversation felt like picking up exactly where they left off — as if the year in between had just been the universe making them wait for the right moment.

Since Then · Every Single Day

Two people who found each other — twice

From a Facebook search to a face-to-face introduction. From strangers to something much more. Their story did not happen overnight — it happened slowly, quietly, in the most honest way possible. And today, on her birthday, every one of those moments is worth remembering. Because they all led here.
